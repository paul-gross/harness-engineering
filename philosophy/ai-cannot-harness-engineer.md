# AI cannot harness engineer

Harness Engineering, at its core, must be done by Humans, not LLMs.

## What?

High level and high order thinking are not within the capabilities of the modern LLM models.

As of writing this, Opus 4.6 is well regarded as an extremely intelligent and capable LLM model that can solve very complex tasks.  We use it to write software and everyone is going through these moments of realization that AI is *insane* (or whatever term it is that they use to show their disbelief / acknowledgement).  It's true, Opus 4.6 can tackle most programming tasks and write code.  It's incredible.  It wowed me in May of 2025.  It wows me today.

Opus 4.6 is not a model that can execute high order thinking.  It can't build a harness for a software application.  If you tell it to build a harness, you'll get *something*, but you probably won't get something valuable.

AI is exceptional at writing something out that has a lot of tokens / words.  It is not exceptional at thinking at the critical level that is needed to push through the frontier of the AI revolution we are in.

## Everything must anchor to human intelligence

My philosophy, my blog, the concepts I discuss, the ideas I come up with -- they are all drawn from my cognition.  I think and I write.  LLMs have not been able to successfully write about harness engineering.  They aren't effective at assisting me in this content.  They struggle because they always want to embellish it with what they *know*.  They always want to add their *thoughts*.  They want to AI'ify it.  And AI'ification when comprehension is not there drifts like crazy.  It *washes it out* with the [average content of the internet](../concepts/average-content-of-the-internet.md).

This is why all of this content is as close to human output as I can tolerate (I just can't pass on having AI fix grammar and spelling, give me a break).

Furthermore, humans are highly adept at spotting and discarding content that they suspect is AI written.  This validates my own philosophy:  We've all learned very quickly that AI does not have high order thinking and thus we have recognized patterns that we can identify as "This is obviously not coming from high order thinking."

I have tried using Opus 4.6 to help me write this content, but it does not work.  I reserve it for only the basics of quality checks.

And yes, sometimes I do write like AI in that I write markdown files.  This is because I've learned that we are being exceptionally well trained at taking in, skimming, and reading markdown content that AI delivers.
